Why Choose Us

Why We're the Everglades' Most Experienced Kayak Outfitter

Mangrove Tunnel Access

We paddle into the largest mangrove forest in the northern hemisphere — dense canopies of bromeliads, orchids, and wildflowers accessible only by kayak.

Alligator Encounters

Our kayaks are stealthy and quiet. We regularly observe alligators from a safe, respectful distance that no power boat could ever achieve.

Master Naturalist Guides

All guides are formally trained Florida Master Naturalists, biologists, botanists, and outdoor educators with 25+ years in this specific ecosystem.

Orchids & Wildflowers

The mangrove canopy hosts an extraordinary collection of native orchids, bromeliads, and wildflowers. Your guide will identify every species you encounter.

National Park Access

We operate inside Everglades National Park and the 10,000 Islands National Wildlife Refuge — protected ecosystems that cannot be accessed any other way.

All Ages & Experience Levels

No kayaking experience required. Our stable kayaks and expert instruction make this accessible for children, seniors, and first-time paddlers.

Our Difference

Why Choose Everglades Kayaking?

If you’re searching for an Everglades kayak tour, you’re not going to find a team with more experience in this specific ecosystem. We’ve been conducting tours in Everglades National Park since the 1990s — which means our guides have paddled these waters thousands of times and know every alligator sunning spot, every orchid tree, and every wildlife pattern by heart.

Our kayaks are stealthy in a way no power boat can be. That stealthiness is what allows us to observe alligators, otters, and birds from a close, respectful distance that simply isn’t possible from any other type of vessel. When you paddle quietly through a mangrove tunnel with an orchid canopy overhead and an alligator resting on the bank 15 feet away, you understand why this experience is unlike anything else in Florida.

This is not exercise — it is purely a means to access some of the most incredibly special places in America. No experience required. All ages welcome. All of our guides are formally trained biologists, botanists, engineers, photographers, or outdoor leaders dedicated to the experiential education of every guest who steps into our kayaks.
